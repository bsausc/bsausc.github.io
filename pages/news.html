News & Events  

BSA successfully organized the first Inter Student Friendship Soccer League (ISFSL) 2011. The final was held between Iran and Turkey on November 12, 2011 at 11:30AM in Storm Thurmond outdoor field#3. Turkey won the championship by defeating Iran 2 goals to 1. Lutfi scored both the goals for Turkey and Amir scored the only goal for Iran. Lutfi from the Turky team was the highest scorer of the tournament with 10 goals. Mr. Herbert Camp, Director of Campus Recreation presented trophy to the Champion and Runner up teams. BSA advisor Dr. Jasim Imran was present during the event.

BSA collected 203 lb of various types of cloth items in the recent Cloth Drive. The collected cloths were sent in 6 boxes to the Humanitarian Delivery Flight To Bangladesh program. BSA advisor Jasim Imran and BSA alumni Md. Monirul Islam and Muhammad Qazi paid the charges for packaging and shipping. BSA thanks all the doners and volunteers for making the Cloth Drive a success.

BSA along with Bangladeshi community in South Carolina celebrated EID Reunion, 2011 at Capstone House, USC on 15th October, 2011. The event featured cultural program by BSA members along with performances from community members. BSA presented a video, One Day of BTV, which was created by BSA members. Find the video here.
Find the complete Eid Reunion program in our Youtube Channel

BSA Fresher's Reception, 2011 was held on September 10, 2011 at the South Quad Lounge, USC. Faculty members and BSA members along with their families were present in the program. The program featured a wonderful cultural show presented by the BSA members.
View Program Video

Bangladeshi students @ USC along with the Bangladeshi community served iftar on 21st August, 2011 at Masjid-al-Muslemin, Columbia, SC. There were arrangements for 250 persons for the iftar. Thanks to everyone for providing help, support and also for the participation.

BSA Cricket Team played a friendly match with Indian Student Orgnanization (ISO) Cricket Team on July 23, 2011. BSA won the match by 72 runs. It was a 16 over match. ISO won the toss and chose to field. BSA made 142/6 in 16 overs. In reply, ISO was all out for 70 runs.

BSA officially said goodbye to Muhammad Qazi (Tonmoy) from Department of Electrical Engineering, USC on 27th June, 2011. Qazi has completed his PhD and is joining Intel Corporation at Portland, Oregon . BSA wishes all the best to Qazi for a successful future.

The annual BSA picnic was held on Sunday, June 5, 2011 @ Weston Lake Recreation Area,SC. Bangladeshi student along with faculty members at USC participated at the picnic. The participants enjoyed the evening with boating, canoeing and vollyball. BBQ chicken with nan and salad were served for dinner at the picnic.
View Photos

BSA in collaboration with the Bangladeshi community living in greater South Carolina organized the 21st February & 26th March celebration commemorating the International Mother language day and Independence Day. The program was held on the evening of April 9, 2011 at the Capstone House, USC.The program featured cultural performances by Bangladeshi community.

BSA along with Bangladeshi community @ South Carolina participated at the Columbia International Festival 2011. The festival was held at the State Fair Ground, Columbia, SC from April 1 - 3, 2011. Bangladeshi community also featured a food stall along with the exhibition stall. Jahangir participated at Parade of the Nations and three beautifully choreographed dance were presented at the festival. Tonfi, Rabbi, Faisal, Imon and Sakib volunteered at the exhibition stall along with BSA committee members to make the event a success.

BSA officially said goodbye to Md. Monirul Islam from Department of Electrical Engineering, USC on 8th January, 2011. Monir completed his PhD in 2009 and is joining Intel Corporation at Portland, Oregon . BSA wishes all the best to Monir for a successful future.

BSA officially said goodbye to M Anas Boksh Mazady from Department of Electrical Engineering, USC on 6th January, 2011. Anas has completed his Masters and is going to University of Connecticut to pursue his PhD. BSA wishes all the best to Anas for a successful future.
